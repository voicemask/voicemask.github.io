VSMask: Defending Against Voice Synthesis Attack via Real-Time Predictive Perturbation

Voice synthesis attack is threatening the security of everyone's voice. How to prevent the attackers from synthesizing our voices is the key question this research aims to answer. In this paper, we explore the feasibility of real-time protection against voice synthesis. By implementing real-time predictive model and universal perturbation header, VSMask successfully protects real-time speech signal in both online and offline real-world scenarios. Furthermore, VSMask mitigates the audio distortion in protected speech via the weight-based amplitude constrained mechanism. We evaluate VSMask protection performance under different real-world scenarios, and the experiment results show that VSMask can effectively defend against voice synthesis attacks in both digital and physical worlds.

Application Scenarios of VSMask Protection

Real Time Communications Applications: It is common for people to send voice memos or make voice calls using real-time communication apps. The attackers may hide in chat groups and record your voice for voice synthesis. Meanwhile, many people meet online since the COVID-19 pandemic, the attackers can attend public meetings and record the victim's voice without permission. However, existing defense methods cannot provide protection for the real-time streaming speech. VSMask can provide real-time adversarial perturbation for the real-time streaming speech.

Social Network: People are willing to share their daily life on social media. There are billions of active users uploading their self-made videos to TikTok, YouTube and Instagram. Usually, all these videos are public and any visitor has the access to download and reuse them. Once you expose your voice in these videos, malicious attackers could clone your voice and impersonate your identity. Even as existing defense methods may be able to protect your voice in videos, it takes much more time than the length of the video itself. VSMask could protect your voice in your social network secure without any extra latency.

Real-world Conversation: Real-world human speech is threatened by voice synthesis attackers as well. While people are talking, an adversary can record the speech audio and launch the voice synthesis attack. Since real-world speech is a streaming audio signal, existing defenses cannot mask our voice efficiently enough to allow the protection of streaming audios. VSMask is able to protect your real-world conversation via real-time over-the-air perturbation injection.

VSMask System Design

  • How to generate real-time perturbation?

    As depicted in above figure, VSMask predictive model can forecast adversarial perturbation for upcoming streaming speech. For example, the model captures the input for time 0 to time t, and outputs the perturbation from t+Δt to t+ Δt+γ . Then the model will slid forward to the next time window and so on. However, the beginning of the speech is still exposed to threat only with the predictive model. To address this challenge, we design a universal perturbation header for the blank time window from 0 to t+Δt for the the specific speaker to completely protect his or her voice against unauthorized speech synthesis attacks.

    Key Features of VSMask:

    • Real-time: The input of the predictive model is the previous speech signal and the output perturbation works for the following speech stream. VoiceMask can generate the perturbation in very short time delay (tens of milliseconds).
    • Imperceptible: VSMask perturbation is operated on the human insensitive hearing frequency band, so that it it more difficult to realize the perturbation in the protected speech.
    • Privacy-preserving: The VSMask model is light-weighted and locally trained. The users do not need to upload their voice to cloud and it keeps their privacy secure.
